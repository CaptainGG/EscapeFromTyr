# Station Escape Narrative

## High Concept
A civilian transport station has gone dark after a cascading systems failure. Security drones still obey the last lockdown directive: contain every moving life sign. The player is a lone survivor trying to cross the station, steal clearance, and catch the final evacuation route.

## Player Role
The player is not a soldier or station commander. They are an ordinary passenger or contractor who knows just enough to use a sidearm and exploit access controls under pressure.

## World And Setting
Station Escape unfolds inside an aging orbital station whose polished transit spaces have degraded into sealed corridors, dead terminals, and emergency lighting. The vertical slice focuses on three linked spaces: an arrival corridor, a security junction, and a shuttle bay at the edge of the evacuation route.

## Tone And Emotional Texture
- Isolated
- Pressurized
- Clinical
- Dangerous
- Determined

The game should feel tense and readable, with just enough atmosphere to make the station feel haunted by automation rather than crowded with lore.

## Core Conflict
The station still has power in fragments, but its safety systems have collapsed into hostility. Every door, drone, and clearance rule stands between the player and escape.

## Key Archetypes
- Survivor: the player, under-equipped but adaptable
- Security Drone: the station's hostile automated enforcer
- Silent Station Network: an unseen authority that still opens and closes routes based on access level

## Forces And Factions
The first slice only needs two active forces:
- The player trying to escape alive
- The station's damaged security infrastructure trying to contain them

## Story Delivery Method
Use concise menu copy, HUD objectives, environmental framing, and a few location labels. Avoid dialogue systems or long narrative text in the first slice.

## Narrative Constraints
- Keep lore light and practical.
- Do not introduce named NPCs in v1.
- Avoid faction sprawl.
- Let the room sequence and objective chain deliver most of the narrative momentum.

## Narrative Support For The Loop
The player fires because drones attack on sight, collects the keycard because the station exit is still governed by clearance, and escapes because staying in place means eventual containment. Every core mechanic directly supports the fiction of breaking out.
